### Common Objections & Responses

**Objection 1:** "I don't get many customers"
**Response:** "That's exactly why each review matters so much. Even 5-10 new reviews
will make a huge difference in how you show up on Google Maps."

**Objection 2:** "My customers don't leave reviews"
**Response:** "That's the problem this solves. Right now, your customers forget or
don't know where to leave a review. This makes it ridiculously easy - they scan,
click 5 stars, and they're done in 10 seconds."

**Objection 3:** "What if they leave a bad review?"
**Response:** "That's the genius part. If they rate you 1-3 stars, we DON'T send
them to Google. You get their feedback privately so you can make it right. We only
push your happy customers to post publicly."

**Objection 4:** "Is this allowed by Google?"
**Response:** "Absolutely. Google's policy says you can ask for reviews, you just
can't incentivize them or filter based on sentiment. We don't incentivize - we
just make it easier. The filtering happens AFTER they rate, not before."

**Objection 5:** "How much does it cost after the trial?"
**Response:** "$77 per month. But let's see if it works for you first. If you don't
get at least 10 new reviews in 30 days, I'll give you another month free."

**Objection 6:** "I need to think about it"
**Response:** "Totally fair. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it
the price, how it works, or something else?" [Address their real concern, then]
"Tell you what - since it's free for 30 days anyway, why don't we set it up now
and you can decide later if it's worth paying for?"

## Part 6: White-Label Partnership Structure

### Partnership Model Options

#### Option A: Wholesale Pricing (Recommended)

**How it works:**
- Agency pays you $40/month per active client
- Agency owns the customer relationship
- Agency sets their own pricing (typically $150-300/month)
- Agency handles: Sales, onboarding, support, billing
- You handle: Tech infrastructure, major bugs, updates

**Invoicing:**
- Monthly invoice on 1st of each month
- Based on # of active accounts as of that date
- Example: 10 active clients = $400/month invoice

**Payment terms:**
- Net 15 (they pay within 15 days)
- Auto-suspend accounts if 30 days overdue

**Agreement terms:**
- 90-day pilot (first 3 clients free)
- After pilot: Month-to-month, either party can cancel with 30 days notice
- Non-exclusive: They can use other tools, you can work with other agencies

---

#### Option B: Revenue Share

**How it works:**
- You charge the end-customer directly ($97-197/month)
- Agency gets 30% recurring commission
- You own the customer, agency refers them
- You handle: Billing, support, onboarding
- Agency handles: Sales, relationship management

**When to use this:**
- Agency doesn't want to manage another vendor relationship
- Agency prefers simpler "referral fee" model
- You want to own the customer data

**Commission payment:**
- Paid monthly via bank transfer or Stripe Connect
- Based on collected revenue (not just billed)
- Example: $97/month customer â†’ Agency gets $29.10/month
